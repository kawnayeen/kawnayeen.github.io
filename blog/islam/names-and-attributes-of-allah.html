Understanding The Names and Attributes of Allah


In the Name of Allah, Most Gracious, Most Merciful

Allah says (interpretation of the meaning) :

It is He who has sent down to you, [O Muhammad], the Book; in it are verses [that are] precise - they are the foundation of the Book - and others unspecific. As for those in whose hearts is deviation [from truth], they will follow that of it which is unspecific, seeking discord and seeking an interpretation [suitable to them]. And no one knows its [true] interpretation except Allah . But those firm in knowledge say, "We believe in it. All [of it] is from our Lord." And no one will be reminded except those of understanding.

Ali' Imran (3:7)

So, those who has disease in his heart play around the unspecific verses of Al-Quran and deviate from the religion.

Now Allah describe himself clearly in the following verses :-

Say, "He is Allah , [who is] One; Allah , the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born, Nor is there to Him any equivalent."

Al Ikhlas (112:1-4)

And in the greatest verse of the Quran, Allah has described his attributes in this way :-

Allah! La ilaha illa Huwa (none has the right to be worshipped but He), the Ever Living, the One Who sustains and protects all that exists. Neither slumber, nor sleep overtakes Him. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is he that can intercede with Him except with His permission? He knows what happens to them (His creatures) in this world, and what will happen to them in the Hereafter. And they will never compass anything of His Knowledge except that which He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and He feels no fatigue in guarding and preserving them. And He is the Most High, the Most Great.

Al Baqarah (2:255)

Now, if we look at the following verses which affirms that Allah has Face, Eyes, Hands :-

And the Face of your Lord full of Majesty and Honour will abide forever.

Ar-Rahman (55:27)

Everything will be destroyed except His Face

Al-Qasas (28:88)

The Jews say: "Allah's Hand is tied up (i.e. He does not give and spend of His Bounty)." Be their hands tied up and be they accursed for what they uttered. Nay, both His Hands are widely outstretched. He spends (of His Bounty) as He wills.

Al-Ma'idah (5:64)

So wait patiently (O Muhammad (ﷺ)) for the Decision of your Lord, for verily, you are under Our Eyes , and glorify the Praises of your Lord when you get up from sleep.

At-Tur (52:48)

And We carried him on a (ship) made of planks and nails, Floating under Our Eyes, a reward for him who had been rejected!

Al-Qamar (54:13,14)

And I endued you with love from Me, in order that you may be brought up under My Eye,

Ta-Ha (20:39)

So, when people go through these verses, where the modality is unknown, they deviate mainly in 2 way :-

  • Doing Tahreef i.e. leaving out actual meaning understood by the specific word & take a meaning which the words may indicate in a doubtful sense. For example, saying Hand of Allah means Power of Allah
  • Doing Ta'teel i.e. denying the real meaning of the word. For example saying Allah don't have any Face.
Position of Ahlus Sunnah wal Jama'ah in this regards : -
  • Believing in those Attributes with which Allah has qualified Himself and with which the Prophet (ﷺ) has qualified Allah.
  • Making no alteration of actual meaning
  • Stay away from denying the actual meaning
  • Stay away from making likeness to creation

I'll end this article with a narration regarding Imam Malik which clearly demonstrate the above mention stance of Ahlus Sunnah wal Jama'ah regarding name and attributes of Allah --

Abu Nu'aym relates from Ja'far Ibn Abdullah who said, "We were with Malik Ibn Anas, so a man came, so he said, 'O Aba 'Abdullah, "The Most Merciful has ascended above His Throne" [Surah Taha 20:5]. How has He ascended?' So anger came over Malik, such that no affair like this had ever caused to come over him. So he looked towards the earth and scratched with his cane in his hand, until he raised his hand and wiped the sweat from his forehead, tossed the cane aside, and said, "The modality (kayhf) of it cannot be comprehended by the intellect, and al-istiwa' (ascent) is not unknown, and having faith in it is obligatory, and the question concerning it is an innovation. And I think you are a person of innovation.' and he commanded him to leave."

The Creed of the Four Imaams, page 37-38
